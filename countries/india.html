India

Well, it's my home country. What more can I say? I was born here, was raised here, was shaped here (both personality-wise and physically). After moving to Sweden since 2022 and a couple of visits back home, I realised that I had been living inside one of the most densely packed experiments in culture, climate, and controlled chaos on the planet.

Most of my memories orbit around Karnataka, and more specifically Bengaluru. Officially, it’s the “Silicon Valley of India.” Unofficially, it’s a place where you can discuss machine learning over artisanal coffee while being stuck in traffic that appears to follow no known laws of fluid dynamics. The city runs on code, caffeine, and an almost philosophical tolerance for unpredictability. Roads are suggestions. Weather forecasts are optimistic guesses. Yet somehow, everything functions.

Karnataka itself is far more than the city. Step outside Bengaluru and the pace shifts. You get coastline, hills, temples carved out of stone with unreasonable precision, and food that quietly asserts dominance without needing explanation. There is a certain engineering elegance to the cuisine here. Simple ingredients, but optimized over generations. Dosa is not just breakfast, it’s a system.

India, as a whole, resists clean summarization. It scales in every dimension. Population, languages, contradictions. You can travel a few hundred kilometers and feel like you’ve crossed into a different country entirely. And yet, there is a strange coherence beneath it all. A shared tolerance for complexity. An ability to operate in systems that look, from the outside, completely unstable.

It’s noisy. It’s inefficient. It’s brilliant. It’s exhausting. It’s home.
