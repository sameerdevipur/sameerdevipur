Sweden

When I moved to Sweden in 2022, my entire understanding of the world was built around Indian chaos and common sense. All of that came crashing down. Sweden operates on efficient rule-following and structure, and it does not apologise for it.

I am not going to be a typical NRI and spend time comparing both societies since that is what children do. But the stark difference is hard to ignore. Here, people follow the rules to the tee, even if it breaks common sense. A driving instructor will tell you to drive into another car if it means you are following the rules and the other driver is not. "It is not your problem, we will win the case in court" is the advice you get from a safety instructor.

Somewhere in that sentence, something feels off. Safety should mean avoiding the crash. Not confidently participating in it because legally you are correct. But Sweden does not optimise for instinct. It optimises for system consistency.

And to be fair, the math makes sense. If everyone follows the rules, everything works perfectly. No ambiguity. No negotiation. Just pure, clean execution.

And the weird part is, it actually works.

Public transport runs on time like it has something to prove. In my experience though, major railways like SJ, Pendeltåg and Öresundståg are unpredictable like a Windows update in the middle of a presentation. But when they do work, they work just fine.

People stand in queues without anyone telling them to. No one cuts lines. No one argues. Silence in public spaces is maintained like a shared contract signed at birth. You stand too close to someone at a bus stop and suddenly you feel like you violated an unwritten clause in Swedish law. No one says anything, but the discomfort is immediate and mutual.

Cities like Stockholm and Malmö feel less like cities and more like controlled chaos (an oxymoron, yes). Everything is spaced, intentional, almost algorithmic. Even IKEA feels like a national personality trait. Minimal, functional, efficient, and slightly confusing until you figure out how the pieces fit together.

Social interactions follow the same logic. No one will interfere in your life unnecessarily. No one will randomly start a conversation. But the moment something genuinely requires help, the response is immediate and efficient. It is not cold. It is just calibrated.

Then winter hits. And this is not a gentle seasonal transition. This is a system stress test. Darkness, cold, silence. The country does not stop, it just downshifts. People go into survival mode with candles, coffee, and fika, which is less a break and more a coping mechanism at that point.

And then summer shows up like a plot twist. Sunlight exists for unreasonable amounts of time, people are outside, smiling, alive. The same society that felt completely muted in winter looks like it collectively rebooted.

So yes, the theory holds. If everyone follows the rules, everything works. Sweden is basically running that experiment at full national scale.

And somehow, it almost always works.
