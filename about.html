About me

I see myself as someone who is constantly curious and mildly allergic to simple answers. Professionally, I am a PhD student in Physics working on Battery Safety and Energy Systems, which mostly means poking complex systems until they reveal how they behave.

My academic background is in Chemical Engineering, so I tend to think in terms of processes, materials, and why things fail in interesting ways. In my free time, I track game progress, write notes that may or may not turn into proper ideas, take photos of things that catch my eye, and collect fun facts that serve no immediate purpose but feel important anyway.

This site is less a formal biography and more an honest snapshot of how I think, work, and occasionally overthink.
