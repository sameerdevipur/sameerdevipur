Side quests

This page is a loose collection of games I have played, movies and shows that stuck with me, and a growing pile of miscellaneous trivia that did not fit anywhere else.

Nothing here is ranked or curated too carefully. It is simply a snapshot of what I enjoy, what I revisit, and the small distractions that quietly shape how I think and unwind.

Games That Changed My Thinking

Not favorites. Systems that altered how I think about structure, strategy, and consequence.

Red Dead Redemption 2

Systems realism · Moral friction · Environmental storytelling · Exploration

I usually never say one game or one movie is a particular favourite, but this one is an exception. RDR2 is easily a game I'd recommend every gamer to play. Hell, I'd even recommend people to get into gaming just to experience this masterpiece.

Mechanic worth stealing: Don't rush and explore in the first hour. Let the game introduce you slowly. Take your absolute sweet time to explore everything later on before finishing the game.

Assassin’s Creed Series

Historical reconstruction · Parkour systems · Iterative design · Amazing trailers

Used to be an amazing series with great gameplay and amazing storylines. They really took the whole "Free-will vs. Order" concept and rocked it. And then they messed it up. In my opinion, Valhalla was the last good AC game and everything is shit after that. Hopefully they will pick up and improve.

Mechanic worth stealing: Use environment as curriculum. Let architecture teach the player.
